## 2. Filing a DMCA Takedown Notice

### Requirements (17 U.S.C. ยง 512(c)(3))

To file a valid DMCA takedown notice, you must provide a written communication that includes:

1. **Physical or electronic signature** of the copyright owner or authorized agent

2. **Identification of the copyrighted work** claimed to be infringed
   - If multiple works, a representative list

3. **Identification of the infringing material** and information reasonably sufficient to locate it
   - Direct URL(s) to the content on VidChain
   - Video title(s) and/or NFT listing ID(s)

4. **Contact information** of the complaining party
   - Name, address, telephone number, and email address

5. **Good faith statement** that the use is not authorized by the copyright owner, its agent, or the law

6. **Accuracy statement** under penalty of perjury that the information is accurate and you are authorized to act on behalf of the copyright owner

### Submission Methods

- **Online Form**: https://vidchain.io/dmca/submit (preferred)
- **Email**: dmca@vidchain.io
- **Mail**: [Physical address]

### Template Notice

```
DMCA TAKEDOWN NOTICE

I, [Your Name], am the copyright owner (or authorized agent for [Copyright Owner Name]) of the following work(s):

[Description of copyrighted work(s)]

The following material on VidChain infringes my copyright:
[URL(s) of infringing content]

Contact Information:
Name: [Your Full Name]
Address: [Your Address]
Phone: [Your Phone Number]
Email: [Your Email Address]

I have a good faith belief that the use of the described material in the manner complained of is not authorized by the copyright owner, its agent, or the law.

I swear, under penalty of perjury, that the information in this notification is accurate and that I am the copyright owner or am authorized to act on behalf of the owner of an exclusive right that is allegedly infringed.

Signature: ___________________
Date: ___________________
```

## 3. Counter-Notification Process

If you believe your content was removed by mistake or misidentification, you may file a counter-notification.

### Requirements (17 U.S.C. ยง 512(g)(3))

A valid counter-notification must include:

1. **Physical or electronic signature** of the user

2. **Identification of the material** that was removed and its location before removal
   - Original URL(s) or content identifiers

3. **Statement under penalty of perjury** that you have a good faith belief the material was removed by mistake or misidentification

4. **Your name, address, and telephone number**

5. **Consent to jurisdiction** of federal court in your district (or if outside the US, any judicial district where VidChain may be found)

6. **Statement** that you will accept service of process from the original complainant

### Counter-Notification Template

```
DMCA COUNTER-NOTIFICATION

I, [Your Name], state under penalty of perjury that I have a good faith belief that the material identified below was removed or disabled as a result of mistake or misidentification:

Removed Content:
[Description of removed content]
[Original URL(s) or Video ID(s)]

Reason for Counter-Notification:
[Explain why the removal was in error - e.g., fair use, ownership, license]

I consent to the jurisdiction of the Federal District Court for [your judicial district] and will accept service of process from the person who provided the original DMCA notification or their agent.

Contact Information:
Name: [Your Full Name]
Address: [Your Address]
Phone: [Your Phone Number]
Email: [Your Email Address]

Signature: ___________________
Date: ___________________
```

### Process After Counter-Notification

1. VidChain forwards the counter-notification to the original complainant
2. Complainant has **10-14 business days** to file court action
3. If no court action is filed, content may be restored after 14 business days
4. If court action is filed, content remains removed pending court resolution

## 4. VidChain Response Procedures

### Upon Receiving Valid Takedown Notice

| Step | Action | Timeline |
|------|--------|----------|
| 1 | Acknowledge receipt | 24 hours |
| 2 | Review notice for completeness | 1-2 business days |
| 3 | Remove or disable access to content | Expeditiously (typically 24-48 hours) |
| 4 | Notify the user who posted the content | Same day as removal |
| 5 | Log the takedown for repeat infringer tracking | Immediately |

### Upon Receiving Valid Counter-Notification

| Step | Action | Timeline |
|------|--------|----------|
| 1 | Acknowledge receipt | 24 hours |
| 2 | Forward to original complainant | Promptly |
| 3 | Wait for court filing notice | 10-14 business days |
| 4 | Restore content (if no court action) | After 14 business days |

### Content Status During DMCA Process

- **Takedown filed**: Content hidden, NFT sales paused
- **Counter-notification filed**: Content remains hidden during waiting period
- **Restored**: Content visible, NFT sales resume
- **Court action pending**: Content remains hidden

## 5. Repeat Infringer Policy

VidChain maintains a policy for terminating accounts of repeat infringers.

### Strike Tracking

| Strikes | Action |
|---------|--------|
| 1st valid DMCA | Warning + content removal |
| 2nd valid DMCA | 30-day upload restriction |
| 3rd valid DMCA | Account termination |

### Considerations

- Counter-notifications that result in restoration do not count as strikes
- Egregious or willful infringement may result in immediate termination
- Strikes expire after 12 months of good standing
- Account termination includes:
  - Removal of all content
  - Freeze of marketplace activity
  - NFT ownership remains on blockchain but platform features disabled

## 6. NFT-Specific Considerations

### Content Removal vs. NFT Ownership

- DMCA takedown **removes platform visibility** of the video
- NFT **ownership on blockchain is immutable** and cannot be removed by VidChain
- NFT **marketplace listing is suspended** during DMCA process
- NFT **metadata on IPFS** may be flagged but cannot be deleted from decentralized storage

### Remedies for NFT Copyright Claims

1. **Platform Removal**: Video hidden from VidChain platform
2. **Marketplace Suspension**: NFT cannot be listed or sold on VidChain marketplace
3. **Metadata Warning**: Warning label added to NFT metadata where possible
4. **Buyer Notification**: Existing NFT owners notified of claim

### Smart Contract Considerations

- Original creator royalties continue per smart contract terms
- Platform cannot modify on-chain ownership or royalty settings
- Off-chain licensing terms may be invalidated by valid DMCA claim

## 7. Misrepresentation Liability

### Warning to Complainants

Under 17 U.S.C. ยง 512(f), any person who knowingly materially misrepresents:
- That material is infringing, or
- That material was removed by mistake

**May be liable for damages**, including costs and attorney's fees incurred by VidChain or the alleged infringer.

### Common Fair Use Considerations

Before filing a takedown, consider whether the use may be fair use:
- Commentary or criticism
- News reporting
- Educational use
- Parody or satire
- Transformative works
